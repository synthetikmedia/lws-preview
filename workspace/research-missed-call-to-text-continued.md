## Section 7: Technical Risks and Challenges

### 🚨 CRITICAL: SMS Compliance (A2P 10DLC)

This is the **single biggest technical/legal risk** for this business. Get it wrong and carriers block your messages, the FCC fines you, or you get sued. Get it right and it's a competitive moat.

#### What is A2P 10DLC?
A2P (Application-to-Person) 10DLC (10-Digit Long Code) is the regulatory framework that governs businesses sending text messages from standard phone numbers in the US. As of 2024, **all business texting must be registered** through this system. Unregistered traffic gets throttled or blocked by carriers (AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon).

#### Registration Requirements (via Twilio data)

**For Fountainhead as a platform (ISV/CSP):**
- Must register as a **Campaign Service Provider (CSP)** with The Campaign Registry (TCR)
- Must register Fountainhead as a brand
- Must register each customer's campaign use case

**For each customer (Brand registration):**
- **Standard registration:** $44 one-time brand registration + $15/campaign vetting fee + $1.50-$10/campaign/month
- **Low-volume standard:** $4 one-time brand registration + $15/campaign vetting fee + $1.50-$10/campaign/month (for <6,000 segments/day)
- **Sole proprietor:** $4 one-time brand registration + $15/campaign vetting fee + $2/campaign/month (for <1,000 messages/day on one number)

**What this means for Fountainhead:**
- You need to build A2P registration INTO the onboarding flow
- Each new customer needs their brand + campaign registered before they can send texts
- Registration approval takes 1-7 business days (sometimes longer)
- This adds friction to the "working in 5 minutes" promise — **mitigation: start registration on signup, use a shared short code or toll-free number for the trial period**

#### Trust Scores and Throughput
After registration, TCR assigns a trust score that determines message throughput:
- **Low trust:** ~75 messages/day (new/unvetted businesses)
- **Medium trust:** ~750 messages/day
- **High trust:** ~2,000-10,000+ messages/day

For missed-call-to-text, most SMBs will need <50 messages/day, so low trust scores are fine initially.

### 🚨 TCPA Compliance (Telephone Consumer Protection Act)

The TCPA is the federal law governing automated communications. Violations carry penalties of **$500-$1,500 per message** (yes, per individual text).

#### Key TCPA Rules for Missed-Call-to-Text:

1. **Prior Express Consent:** The caller initiated contact by calling the business — this generally constitutes implied consent for a single transactional response. However:
   - One response to a missed call = likely OK under "established business relationship" exception
   - Multiple follow-up texts = requires explicit opt-in consent
   - Marketing content in the response = may require prior express WRITTEN consent
   
2. **Safe harbor for Fountainhead's use case:**
   - The customer called first (inbound intent)
   - The text is a direct, timely response to their inquiry
   - It's transactional, not marketing (if worded correctly)
   - **BUT: Do NOT include promotional language, coupons, or marketing in the auto-response.** Keep it purely responsive: "Sorry I missed your call, how can I help?"

3. **Opt-out requirement:** Every text should include an opt-out mechanism (or at minimum, respect "STOP" replies). This is both a legal requirement and carrier requirement.

4. **DNC (Do Not Call) list:** If the business later wants to follow up with marketing texts, the number must be checked against the National DNC registry.

#### TCPA Risk Mitigation:
- Build opt-out handling into the platform (auto-detect "STOP" replies)
- Template messages reviewed by legal counsel
- Hard limit on number of texts per missed call (1 auto-response + AI conversation only if they reply)
- Never send texts to numbers that have opted out — maintain a suppression list
- **Add TCPA compliance as a value prop:** "We keep you compliant so you don't get sued"

### Carrier Filtering and Deliverability

Even with A2P registration, carriers actively filter messages:
- **Content filtering:** Messages that look "spammy" get blocked. Avoid ALL CAPS, excessive links, urgency language.
- **Rate limiting:** Sending too many messages too fast from one number triggers throttling.
- **Number reputation:** If recipients mark your texts as spam, your number's reputation tanks.

**Mitigation:**
- Use dedicated numbers per business (not shared)
- Keep messages short, personal, conversational
- Monitor delivery rates per customer — flag issues proactively
- Rotate sending patterns to avoid burst detection

### Technical Architecture Risks

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| **Twilio/Vonage outage** | Medium | Multi-provider setup (Twilio primary, Bandwidth backup). Or use Twilio + Telnyx for redundancy. |
| **AI hallucination** | High | The AI texts wrong information (wrong hours, wrong services). Mitigation: constrain AI to known facts only, require business owner to approve AI knowledge base. |
| **Latency** | Medium | Text needs to arrive within 30-60 seconds of missed call. If infrastructure is slow, the moment passes. Mitigation: edge functions, pre-compiled responses, <5 second target. |
| **Scaling phone number provisioning** | Low | Each customer needs a number or port their existing. Twilio handles this, but porting takes 1-4 weeks. Offer call-forwarding as instant alternative. |
| **Data privacy** | Medium | You're handling customer phone numbers and conversation data. Need privacy policy, data retention policy, potential SOC 2 if targeting healthcare/legal verticals. |
| **Cost per message creep** | Low-Medium | Twilio charges ~$0.0079/SMS segment. At scale (100 customers × 30 msgs/day = 3,000/day = ~$23/day = ~$700/mo). Manageable but monitor. |

### State-Level Regulations

Several states have their own texting/privacy laws on top of TCPA:
- **Florida (SB 1120):** Requires prior express written consent for ALL commercial texts, even to existing customers. Stricter than federal TCPA.
- **California (CCPA/CPRA):** Customer data handling requirements, right to deletion.
- **Washington, Oklahoma, others:** Various state telemarketing laws.

**Mitigation:** Build consent collection into the flow. When the customer replies to the auto-text, their reply = express consent for continued conversation. Document everything.
